Travion - B2B Travel Platform Redesign

UX Research UI Design Prototyping

The Problem

The Travacations B2B platform, a critical tool for travel agents, was underperforming. Key metrics revealed a sinking ship: user engagement had plummeted by 40%, and a staggering 60% of booking attempts were being abandoned mid-process. The core issue was a convoluted, 12-step booking workflow that was unintuitive and time-consuming, causing significant friction and frustration for its power users.

My Role

As the Lead UX Designer, I was responsible for the end-to-end redesign. This involved spearheading the user research, defining the new user flow and information architecture, creating wireframes and high-fidelity prototypes, and conducting usability testing with key stakeholders.

The Process

Discovery & Research: The first step was to understand the "why" behind the data. I conducted in-depth interviews with 15 travel agents. The key insight was that agents weren't booking linearly; they were constantly juggling multiple client itineraries, comparing options, and needing to quickly jump between tasks. The existing system wasn't built for this multi-threaded workflow.

Design & Prototyping: I developed a clean, modern design system to bring consistency to the UI. The high-fidelity prototypes focused on clarity and efficiency, with a task-based layout that mirrored the agents' mental models. We prioritized clear visual hierarchy and reduced cognitive load at every step.

Testing & Iteration: We conducted five moderated usability tests with agents using the Figma prototype. The feedback was overwhelmingly positive, but it also revealed a key area for improvement: the initial dashboard felt too cluttered. Based on this, I iterated on the design, introducing a more streamlined card-based system that tested much better in a second round of testing.

The Outcome & Impact

The redesigned platform was a resounding success. By streamlining the booking process from 12 steps down to just 4, we achieved a 70% reduction in the average time to book. This efficiency gain led to a 90% increase in agent satisfaction and, crucially, an 80% drop in booking abandonment. This directly translated to a 25% increase in completed bookings within the first quarter of launch.
