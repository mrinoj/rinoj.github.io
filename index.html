Travion - B2B Travel Platform Redesign

UX Research UI Design Prototyping

The Problem

A critical B2B tool for travel agents was failing. User engagement had plummeted by 40%, and 60% of bookings were abandoned due to a convoluted, 12-step workflow that was causing significant frustration.

01. Discovery & Research

I conducted in-depth interviews with 15 travel agents. The key insight: agents weren't booking linearly; they were constantly juggling multiple client itineraries. The existing system wasn't built for this multi-threaded workflow.

02. Design & Prototyping

I developed a clean design system and high-fidelity prototypes focused on a task-based layout that mirrored the agents' mental models. We prioritized clear visual hierarchy and reduced cognitive load at every step.

03. Testing & Iteration

Moderated usability tests with agents revealed the initial dashboard felt too cluttered. I iterated on the design, introducing a more streamlined card-based system that tested much better in a second round of testing.

From 12 Steps to 4

Step 1: Simplified Search

A clean, intuitive search interface that allows agents to quickly find flights, hotels, and car rentals.

Step 2: Itinerary Builder

A drag-and-drop interface to easily build and manage multiple client itineraries.

Step 3: One-Page Booking

A single-page booking form that consolidates all necessary information, reducing clicks and confusion.

Step 4: Instant Confirmation

A clear, concise confirmation page with all booking details and next steps.

Outcome & Impact

The redesign was a resounding success. By streamlining the booking process from 12 steps down to just 4, we achieved a 70% reduction in booking time, an 80% drop in abandonment, and a 25% increase in completed bookings in Q1.

Bhavanam - Patient Management Platform

Healthcare UX Accessibility (WCAG) Prototyping

The Need

A leading hospital network was struggling with a clunky, outdated patient management system causing significant administrative overhead. The goal was to build a new platform focused on reducing cognitive load for medical staff and ensuring full accessibility compliance.

01. Discovery & Research

I spent two weeks shadowing nurses and doctors on their rounds. This firsthand observation was invaluable. I identified major pain points, such as excessive clicking to access patient vitals and difficulty reading text in poorly lit rooms.

02. Design & Prototyping

Using Figma, I created high-fidelity, interactive prototypes. Every design choice, from color palettes to font sizes, was vetted against WCAG 2.1 AA guidelines. The design featured a dark mode to reduce eye strain during night shifts, a feature specifically requested by nurses.

03. Testing & Iteration

Usability tests with 10 medical professionals confirmed the task-based approach was much faster, but revealed some icons were not immediately intuitive. I iterated on the icon library, adding text labels for clarity, which tested perfectly in the second round.

Key Features

Dark Mode

A high-contrast dark mode to reduce eye strain for medical staff during long night shifts.

Task-Based Dashboard

An intuitive dashboard that surfaces the most critical patient information and tasks upfront.

WCAG 2.1 AA Compliance

Ensuring the platform is usable by people with a wide range of disabilities.

Result & Impact

The new system was a resounding success. We measurably reduced administrative time by 50%, freeing up hundreds of hours per week. This meant medical staff could dedicate more valuable time to what truly matters: patient care.

OurShoppee - E-Commerce Experience

Conversion Optimization A/B Testing UI Design

The Problem

A successful e-commerce brand faced a classic "leaky funnel": high traffic but a low conversion rate of just 1.5%. Analytics pointed to a high cart abandonment rate, with the multi-page checkout process being the primary culprit.

01. Discovery & Research

Using Hotjar, I analyzed heatmaps and session recordings of over 200 user sessions. I discovered that users were dropping off significantly when asked to create an account and when presented with unexpected shipping costs late in the process.

02. Design & Prototyping

I designed two primary challengers in Figma for A/B testing: one with a prominent guest checkout and another with a collapsible one-page accordion layout. Both designs brought shipping information forward in the flow.

03. Testing & Iteration

We ran the A/B tests over two weeks. The version with the prominent guest checkout and accordion layout was the clear winner, showing a statistically significant lift in conversions.
