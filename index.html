UX Found Me

Graduated Engineering in Computer Science in 2020, but somewhere along the way, I realised my future wasn’t just lines of code. Late-night college projects introduced me to something I didn’t expect: User Experience Design. It was everything I loved rolled into one—people, pixels, and problem-solving. From that moment, I knew I was all in.

Like many others, my career began at home during the pandemic. While the world slowed down, I doubled down on learning—UX Design, Psychology, and Visual Design became my foundation. My very first gig came from a client in the US, which meant late-night calls, endless revisions, and a lot of caffeine. Oddly enough, I enjoyed every moment of it.

In 2022, the UAE’s tech scene was booming, and I couldn’t resist being a part of it. Having studied in Abu Dhabi and with family already there, the move felt natural. I joined Bankiom, a promising fintech startup backed by 500 Global, where I got to design at the intersection of finance and innovation.

The following year, something unexpected happened. One of my freelance clients, OurShopee, returned with a full-time offer. It was a hybrid role—the best of both worlds. I took the leap and explored the B2B e-commerce landscape, helping shape their platform and user experience.

In 2024, a friend pitched a bold idea in travel tech. I couldn’t ignore the excitement (or the FOMO), so I jumped in. Together, we built a B2B travel app from scratch—transforming a vision into a real product that could redefine how travel works in India. The adrenaline of startup life? Unmatched.

The startup journey taught me more than I could have imagined—about design, business, and resilience. Now, I’m ready to bring that experience back to the UAE’s dynamic ecosystem. Dubai’s energy and innovation inspire me, and I can’t wait to be part of building what comes next.

Travion

A comprehensive B2B SaaS platform for travel agencies and DMCs.

My Role

Lead UX Designer & Research Coordinator

Timeline

18 Months

Product

B2B SaaS Platform

Tools

Figma, Miro, JS

An Industry Drowning in Chaos

India's $12 billion B2B travel market is booming, yet the people driving it are stuck in the past. My initial research revealed that 78% of travel professionals spend 3-4 hours daily on manual coordination, juggling numerous WhatsApp chats, emails, and phone calls. They were losing money and clients, not because they weren't skilled, but because their tools were fragmented and inefficient. The core problem was clear:

“Travel professionals in India need a single, unified platform that tames their operational chaos, supports their relationship-based way of doing business, and works as well on the move as it does at their desk.”

How Might We...?

How might we design a single platform that empowers travel agents to manage their entire workflow—from discovery to happy clients—and build trust within their network, especially from a mobile device?

01

Foundational Research

I designed a mixed-methods research plan to get both hard data and human stories. We surveyed over 200 professionals across India. The data was stark: 67% had lost bookings due to slow response times, and a massive 84% were using WhatsApp for primary business communication.

This quantified the inefficiency, but shadowing agents in their offices revealed the true chaos. I watched them switch between over 7 different tools to manage one booking.

"I have the client on the phone, the hotel in a WhatsApp chat, and the driver on another call. It's incredibly stressful."
- Priya, DMC Operations Manager
02

Defining the MVP & Core Features

Defining the User & Their Journey

I synthesized the research into two key personas—Rajesh (Agency Owner) and Priya (DMC Manager). To truly empathize, I mapped Priya's journey of handling a flight cancellation. Her current process was a chaotic, 2-hour scramble. This map became a powerful tool to show stakeholders exactly where our product could make the biggest impact.

Ideation & Designing the Core Pillars

I led the team through ideation workshops, which led to three core design pillars for Travion. These pillars directly translate into the key features of the application: A Single Source of Truth, a Digital Trust Network, and an On-the-Go Command Center.

03

Prototyping, Testing & Evolution

Prototyping with Purpose

I made a strategic decision to build our high-fidelity prototype using HTML/CSS/JS. This was critical. A static Figma prototype wouldn't have allowed us to test the complex, data-driven interactions that were core to Travion's value, such as the real-time countdown timers, dynamic profit calculations in the Itinerary Builder, and the real user experience of interacting with the AI features.

Usability Testing & Key Iterations

I conducted 25 moderated usability tests, which led to crucial, data-driven iterations. A confusing Floating Action Button was simplified to the two most essential tasks. After users wanted immediate feedback on their rank in the Trust Hub, I added a direct link to the Leaderboard, which increased engagement by 30%. The positive feedback on AI features validated our investment in AI-powered assistance.

The Solution: A Unified Platform

The final design is a clean, powerful, and intuitive web app that unifies all tasks—from managing bookings to tracking financials—into one role-based dashboard.

On-the-Go Command Center

A key insight was that 84% of agents used WhatsApp for business. They weren't tied to their desks. We built a mobile-first app that wasn't just a "lite" version, but a powerful tool to manage their entire workflow, from AI-powered opportunities to client management, from anywhere.

Impact: Taming Chaos, Driving Growth

The 18-month design journey delivered transformative results, validated by strong metrics from our beta users.

Learnings & Reflections

Prototype for the Problem

Building a functional prototype was more work, but it was the right choice. It allowed us to test complex interactions like the AI features and swipe gestures, leading to far richer insights than a static mockup ever could.

Solve the "Unseen" Problems

Users didn't explicitly ask for an "AI-powered Smart Feed," but by observing their daily struggles, I identified a need for proactive assistance. Solving these unarticulated needs is what separates a good product from a great one.

Enhance Relationships

In a market built on trust, our most successful feature wasn't a tool, but the Trust Hub—a feature designed to digitally replicate and strengthen real-world relationships. This was the key to unlocking user adoption and delight.

A System Drowning in Complexity

OurShopee's internal CRM was a major bottleneck. Teams across marketing, sales, and HR were struggling with a cluttered, one-size-fits-all interface that was inefficient and lacked mobile support. Key workflows were slow, data was hard to find, and user frustration was high. The system was hindering growth, not enabling it.

"Users don't need more features, they need the *right* features. The current CRM overwhelms everyone with information that isn't relevant to their role."

How Might We...?

How might we redesign the internal CRM to be role-based, efficient, and mobile-friendly, empowering each team to perform their tasks without friction?

01

Research & Discovery

Understanding the Users

To understand the core issues, I conducted stakeholder interviews, user surveys, and observational studies. This mixed-methods approach provided both quantitative data on usage patterns and qualitative insights into user frustrations.

Key Findings:

  • Cluttered Interface: Multiple teams expressed a need for streamlined interfaces showing only fields relevant to their tasks.
  • Mobile Desperation: The sales team was desperate for mobile access for quick information retrieval, not complex editing.
  • Workflow Inefficiencies: HR personnel identified major workflow bottlenecks and collaboration challenges within the existing system.
02

Synthesis & Definition

Finding the Structure

Common themes from the research were clear: Personalization, Efficiency, and Collaboration were the top needs. A card sorting exercise with users from marketing, sales, and HR helped define an intuitive, user-generated information architecture.

Defining the Personas

I synthesized this research into three key personas to guide all design decisions:

  • Maria (Marketing): Needs a custom dashboard for campaign metrics.
  • Ahmed (Sales): Needs quick mobile access to customer & order info.
  • Fatima (HR): Needs a task-oriented layout for managing workflows.
03

Architecture & Prototyping

Building the Blueprints

The new design was built on four key architectural pillars: 1) Role-Based Architecture (personalized dashboards), 2) Mobile-First App (quick access for sales), 3) Efficient HR Workflow (task-oriented section), and 4) Responsive Web App (detailed view for power users).

Testing & Iteration

High-fidelity prototypes were created for both web and mobile. Usability testing with representative users was crucial, leading to key iterations that simplified the mobile app's navigation and clarified information hierarchy on the web app for faster task completion.

The Solution: A Role-Based CRM

The final design is a clean, responsive, and role-based platform. Each user (Marketing, Sales, or HR) now gets a personalized dashboard with only the tools they need, dramatically simplifying workflows. The new mobile-first app empowers the sales team to manage customer relations on the go.

Impact: Efficiency & Clarity Restored

By focusing on role-based personalization and mobile access, the redesigned CRM transformed internal operations, turning user frustration into empowerment.

The Challenge: A Leaky Funnel

Bankiom, a promising fintech startup, faced a major hurdle: their website and web app had a low conversion rate and a high bounce rate. The user experience was unintuitive, failing to effectively engage and convert potential customers.

The Solution

Data-Driven Redesign
